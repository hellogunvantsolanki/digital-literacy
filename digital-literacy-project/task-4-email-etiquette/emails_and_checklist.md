## Responsible Social Media Use: Do's and Don'ts

### Do's:
- Do maintain a professional online presence
- Do think before posting anything
- Do respect others' privacy and dignity
- Do verify information before sharing
- Do use privacy settings appropriately

### Don'ts:
- Don't post embarrassing or offensive content
- Don't share others' personal information without permission
- Don't engage in cyberbullying or harassment
- Don't post sensitive academic or financial information
- Don't respond impulsively to controversial topics

## "Reply-All" Mishap Reflection

Scenario: A student accidentally sends a personal complaint about a group project to the entire class mailing list instead of to the instructor.

What happened: The student's critical comment about group members' work ethic became visible to everyone, causing embarrassment and damaging team relationships.

How it could have been avoided: Before hitting send, the student should have verified the recipient list, used the 'to' field carefully instead of 'reply-all', and considered whether the message was appropriate for the intended audience. A simple review of the draft before sending would have prevented this incident.
